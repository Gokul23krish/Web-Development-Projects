Writing Readable Content for the Web

August 27, 2025 · 5 min read

Readable content respects the reader’s time. Clear headings, short paragraphs, and generous spacing set the tone. Smaller design choices—contrast, measure, rhythm—make a big difference.

Lead with structure

Outline your ideas with headings first. Readers should grasp the shape of the article by scanning. Use descriptive, action‑oriented headings that communicate value.

Be concise

Favor short sentences. Trim filler words. Pair text with visuals only when the image actually clarifies the point. Less friction equals more comprehension.

Respect the environment

People read on the move and on small screens. Keep line length comfortable, increase line height a touch, and ensure color contrast meets WCAG standards.

Good content is generous. It gets out of the way and lets readers do what they came to do—learn, decide, or act.
