Typography That Works: Scales and Rhythm

September 22, 2025 · 7 min read

Good typography turns content into an experience. It guides the eye, clarifies structure, and reduces friction. Three practical tools go a long way: a modular type scale, a comfortable measure, and consistent vertical rhythm.

Start with a scale

Choose a base size—often 16px—and a ratio such as 1.2 or 1.25. Use this to derive heading sizes and fine‑tune them with clamp() for responsiveness. This creates a predictable hierarchy that works across breakpoints.

Mind the measure

Keep line length between 45–75 characters for body text. Narrower columns are easier to scan, especially on large screens. On small screens, increase line‑height slightly to avoid dense blocks.

Establish rhythm

Consistent spacing between lines and blocks builds rhythm. Use a spacing scale that relates to your type scale, and avoid random values. Rhythm reduces cognitive load; readers can focus on meaning, not layout.

Typography is never finished, but these techniques help your content feel deliberate and effortless—exactly what readers appreciate.
